# Child Drawing Preservation

## Purpose
Keep Mateo's drawings as the source of truth while adapting them for production-safe gameplay and UI use.

## Trigger Conditions
- The request touches any art polish, cropping, cleanup, tracing, readability pass, or asset optimization based on Mateo's drawings.
- A change risks making the game feel generic, over-smoothed, or commercially polished.

## Scope
- Silhouette preservation, asymmetry retention, crop choices, cleanup limits, and translation of drawing logic into game-ready forms.
- Choosing what to simplify and what must stay awkward, tender, or strange.

## Non-Scope
- Full redesigns, style replacement, faux-premium rendering passes, or realism upgrades.
- Rewriting game systems when the issue is really visual identity.

## Strict Guardrails
- Start from the original drawing, not from the current polished derivative.
- Preserve odd proportions, spoke/branch logic, imperfect symmetry, and tactile line energy.
- Simplify only to improve readability, not to make the art cleaner for its own sake.
- Never remove the trait that makes the drawing unmistakably Mateo's.
- If a polish pass makes the art feel more generic than before, revert and reduce.
- Optimize assets after identity is locked, not before.

## Success Criteria
- The result still reads as Mateo's drawing at first glance.
- Gameplay readability improves without flattening personality.
- Key silhouette and emotional weirdness survive the cleanup.
- The asset remains lightweight enough for mobile runtime use.

## Minimal Output
- What must stay
- What may simplify
- Identity risk
- Final read test
