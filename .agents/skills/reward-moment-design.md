# Reward Moment Design

## Purpose
Make ingredient rewards feel special, readable, and emotionally meaningful without turning them into cutscenes.

## Trigger Conditions
- The request mentions reward, ingredient, ending panel, win screen, victory copy, payoff beat, or end-of-level moment.
- A reward exists but does not feel valuable, legible, or well composed on mobile.

## Scope
- Reward framing, win-state pause/freeze, hierarchy, timing, copy layout, glow/pop timing, and hero reaction.
- Short mobile-first end beats that integrate with the current scene.

## Non-Scope
- Long cinematics, lore exposition, heavy narrative UI, or collectible/inventory systems.
- Replacing the ingredient concept or rewriting the level flow.

## Strict Guardrails
- Stop or calm gameplay under the panel so motion does not fight readability.
- Use a single dominant reward focal point.
- Keep panel layout safe-area-aware and portrait-first.
- Title, label, body, and closing line must each have a clear role.
- Animation should finish fast and feel warm, not sugary or loud.
- Reward visuals must stay coherent with the world, not feel like imported UI.

## Success Criteria
- The reward is instantly legible and feels worth reaching.
- Text does not overlap or compete with gameplay motion underneath.
- The end beat lands emotionally in under three seconds.
- The scene still feels like the same game, not a separate app screen.

## Minimal Output
- Reward focal point
- Sequence timing
- Copy hierarchy
- Mobile fit check
